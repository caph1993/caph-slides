Real world scenarios

Job hiring: Denying jobs

  • Ground truth not available: human decisions
  • Possible masking:
    • hire talented men and nice looking women, or vice versa
    • hire randomly one of the groups for "making up the numbers"
  • Possible intersectionality:
    • do not hire single women
    • do not hire immigrants that live in these places
  • Feedback loop: job experience

Granting loans: Denying loans

  • Ground truth available*
  • Feedback loop: credit history

College admission: Denying admission

  • Possible masking
  • Ground truth not available
  • Feedback loop: previous institutions

Desease likelihood: Suspecting of a minority

  • Ground truth available: medical tests

Criminal risk assessment: Mistrust individual or group

  • Ground truth available

Child maltreatment prediction: Both

  • Proxy variable: neighbors call to report non-white families

Recommender system: Not offering services/jobs

  • No ground truth available

Fairness notions

  1. Group fairness
  2. Individual fairness

Notation:

  • $Y$: true output
  • $\hat Y$: predicted output ($S$: predicted score)
  • $A$: Sensible attribute
  • $E$: Explainable attribute

Group fairness notions

Classification Formulation
Independence $\hat Y \perp A$
or $(\hat Y \perp A) \mid E$
Separation $(\hat Y \perp A) \mid Y$
or $(S \perp A) \mid Y$
or $(\hat Y \perp A) \mid Y=1$
Sufficiency $Y \perp A \mid \hat Y$ or $(S \perp A) \mid \hat Y$

Individual fairness notions

Notion Intuition
Fairness through awareness Similar people $\to$ similar scores
Causal discrimination Almost equal people $\to$ equal scores
Counterfactual What if $A$ had been $a\prime$
No unresolved discrimination All causation paths contain acceptable variables
No proxy dicrimination No causation path contains proxy variables
